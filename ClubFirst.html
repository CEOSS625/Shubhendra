Product Innovation for Social Impact at Club First

Overview

As a Research Associate at Club First, I designed and prototyped over 5 innovative products for real-world applications, working at the intersection of robotics, automation, and social impact. My most significant contribution was the development of the Instant Water Purification and Vending System (IWPVS)—engineered to provide safe drinking water in underserved rural areas of India. This product was developed in collaboration with the Naandi Foundation under Deutsche Bank’s CSR initiative.

Beyond product design, I played multiple roles—including Design Engineer, Product Manager, Liaison Officer, and Sales Executive—to manage end-to-end development, client interaction, and funding proposals.

Multifunctional Product Development

In a dynamic startup environment, I contributed to a wide array of product innovations:

  • IWPVS (Instant Water Purification and Vending System) Developed a rugged, modular water purification and vending solution compatible with rural infrastructure, accounting for:
    • Nonlinear electricity supply
    • Humid environmental conditions
    • Low literacy user base
    • Field deployability and cost constraints

    More than 50 vending machines were deployed across Rajasthan, Delhi, and Haryana. The systems featured NFC-based vending, Reverse Osmosis, 5-stage filtration, and UV water treatment. My role focused on machine design, filtration system development, and integration of components based on specific requirements from Naandi Foundation to serve rural communities.

    My role focused on machine design, filtration system development, and integration of components based on specific requirements from Naandi Foundation to serve rural communities.

    The engineering process included:

    • Product Planning and Requirement Mapping
    • Mechatronics Integration and Modular Design
    • Design for Quality and Field Durability

    Three models (200 LPH, 600 LPH, and 1200 LPH) were created based on location-specific water demand. The project successfully replaced underperforming manual plants and improved sustainability, with 20+ legacy units upgraded using our new design.

    The NFC vending system centralized control and eliminated misuse by routing all transaction data through a GSM-based communication module, enabling low-bandwidth real-time monitoring.

    Note: Embedded electronics and communication systems were developed by Mr. Bhuvanesh Mishra.

  • Home-Serving Robot:Designed and prototyped the robot’s entire mechanical structure, including dynamic joint systems and motion links for multi-tray delivery functionality.
  • Jar Cleaning Machine:Independently developed a jar cleaning machine for 20L water jars, from concept to final product, addressing hygiene needs for IWPVS end users.
  • Electric Golf Kart:Designed a battery-powered electric campus tour vehicle for RCERT (Regional College for Education Research and Technology), Sitapura, with attention to ergonomics, drivetrain, and lightweight chassis.
