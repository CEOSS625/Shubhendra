COVID-19 Data Visualization Project

“Deciphering the Pandemic: A Data-Driven Narrative of Global and Regional Dynamics”

As part of the Data Visualization course at Clemson University, I collaborated with Balvindra Pratap Singh and Nachiket Inamdar to create a highly interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing the spread and response to COVID-19. Our project focused on normalizing global health data to uncover more meaningful comparisons across countries and U.S. states.

Rather than presenting raw numbers, we applied data normalization per 100K people, offering a fair comparison of infection, mortality, and vaccination rates across G7 nations and southeastern U.S. states. We incorporated dynamic filters, highlight actions, tooltips, and time-series visualizations to tell a clearer, more engaging story.

From revealing that the U.S. was not the worst-affected G7 nation (when adjusted for population), to showing how states like South Carolina were unfairly judged without context, our dashboard redefined how pandemic data should be understood.

This dashboard was praised as one of the best in class for adhering to Tuftian principles of visual storytelling, ensuring clarity, consistency, and impact in every chart and comparison.
